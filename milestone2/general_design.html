General Design

Details and Fonts

For this first application, we’re using the University of Victoria colour brandings as laid out on their website. Ideally, each version of the application will be developed with individual post secondary institutions, and each version will be branded to their colour schemes.

The best choices for fonts are the most accessible ones as unofficially defined by the US Department of Health & Human Services: Times New Roman, Verdana, Arial, Tahoma, Helvetica, and Calibri. In order to achieve continuity with the University of Victoria branding, we’ve chosen to use the Calibri font at a minimum of 14 point.

Conceptual Design

The Conceptual Models used in the application design are the map and the AR usage of the camera function of the phone. Other conceptual models are incorporated into this application such as the starting menu consisting of 4 options: search, building directory, directions, and map; the AR mode; and navigation mode.

The starting menu of the application gives the user 4 options. These 4 options are in the shape of apps similar to those on a normal smartphone home screen. The first option is ”search”: a Search Bar appears, similar to a search engine search bar, allowing the user to search for the building they are trying to find. In this screen, the AR mode can also be accessed to identify the building the user is standing near. The second option on the home screen that the user can choose is the “building” function, this function gives a list of the names of all the UVic buildings. The list, and information is similar to a dictionary application showing the information of the building chosen. From this function, the user can choose directions to or from the selected building, to view the floor plans of the selected building, or view the building on the map. The next option is the “directions” option, this is modeled like the map function of any map application. This option allows the users to input their location, and their destination, after which, the user is shown the way from their location to their destination. The last option that can be reached from the main menu of the application is the “map”. The map shows a general map similar to Google maps, allowing the user to scroll around the map, and choose any buildings they want information on.

From the building information screen or the map screen, the ability to turn on the AR mode is available. This option is available as a button on the top right of the screen. The AR function uses phone’s camera allowing the the user to angle it at a nearby building and identify which building it is. From the building identification, the user can view the floorplans of the building, the information of the building, get directions from, or return to the previous menu. Additionally, in the directions menu, an AR mode can be activated. Once a starting point, and destination are entered, this AR navigation mode will overlay a universal arrow on the screen pointing in the direction the user is meant to travel to get to their destination.
