Augmented Reality Campus Navigation

Your own clairvoyance spell

Introduction

Imagine a bustling campus in early September: filled to the brim with students, new and old; faculty; staff; and curious parents. Each person with a different destination, and many overwhelmed. Currently, campuses have billboards near common entrances to the campus with a static map to help these people navigate an often-confusing space. These maps are often outdated, and North is not readily identified.

Now imagine being able to download an application to your mobile device to readily help with this navigation. Certainly, some campuses have mobile applications that seem to have fallen by the wayside in terms of modernity, and maintenance. Imagine an application that allows the user to open a map that identifies, in real time, where on campus they currently are, the ability to rotate the map to better suit the direction theyâ€™re facing, an option to see the floorplan(s) of a building to navigate inside, an augmented reality option to identify a building on sight, searchable building names, and the option for directions between places on campus.
